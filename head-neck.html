Head & Neck Cancer (Nose, Skin, Ear, Salivary Gland)

Head & neck cancers can arise from nose, sinuses, skin, ear, salivary glands, and even the eye. Early diagnosis and expert surgery are key to cure and preservation of function and appearance.

Common Warning Signs

  • Non-healing ulcer or skin lesion
  • Nasal blockage or bleeding
  • Swelling near jaw/ear (salivary gland)
  • Ear discharge or growth
  • Facial swelling, numbness, or eye symptoms

Risk Factors

  • Sun exposure (skin cancers)
  • Tobacco & alcohol
  • Chronic infections
  • Radiation exposure
  • Occupational exposure (wood dust – sinonasal tumors)

Staging

  • TNM classification
  • Final staging after biopsy/surgery (histopathology)

Types & Prognosis

1. Skin Cancer (Face/Neck)

  • Basal cell carcinoma – OS >95%
  • Squamous cell carcinoma – OS 70–90%
  • Melanoma – variable (stage dependent)

2. Nose & Paranasal Sinus Cancer

  • Often late presentation
  • OS: 40–70%

3. Salivary Gland Tumors

  • Benign – excellent outcome
  • Malignant – OS 50–80%

4. Ear Cancer

  • Rare but aggressive
  • OS: 40–60%

5. Eye (Orbital/Ocular Tumors)

  • Advanced tumors may require enucleation or evisceration
  • Early diagnosis can preserve vision

Treatment

  • Biopsy for diagnosis
  • Surgery (main treatment)
  • Reconstruction (cosmetic + functional)
  • Radiotherapy / Chemotherapy

Common Myths

  • “Skin cancer is minor and can be removed anywhere”
    Reality: Improper removal can lead to recurrence and facial deformity.
  • “Any surgeon can operate these cancers”
    Reality: These tumors lie close to eye, nerve, and brain—require specialist expertise.
  • “If it doesn’t pain, it’s not cancer”
    Reality: Many cancers are painless in early stages.
