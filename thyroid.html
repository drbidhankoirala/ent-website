Thyroid Cancer Care

Advanced • Evidence-based • Specialist care

Symptoms

Neck lump
Voice change
Difficulty swallowing
Breathing difficulty
Many patients have no symptoms in early stage.

Clinical Features

Hard thyroid nodule
Neck lymph node
Voice change (nerve involvement)

Diagnosis

Ultrasound (TI-RADS)
FNAC (Bethesda)
NPL to check vocal cord mobility
CT /MRI Scan (in advance cases to check invasion to nearby structures)

Treatment

Hemithyroidectomy
Complications:
• Bleeding (1–2%)
• Voice change (1–3%)
Total Thyroidectomy
Complications:
• Low calcium (20–30%)
• Voice change (1–5%)
• Rare breathing issue
Completion Thyroidectomy
Second surgery done when cancer confirmed after first surgery.
RAI Therapy
Oral treatment after surgery.
Short hospital stay required.
Radiation Therapy
Used in advanced cases.
In advanced disease, airway compromise may require tracheostomy to secure breathing.

Hope & Survival

Thyroid cancer is one of the most curable cancers.
Even after diagnosis, most patients live long, healthy lives with proper treatment.

With modern surgery and follow-up, cure rates are very high. Early diagnosis further improves outcomes.
