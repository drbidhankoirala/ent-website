Warning Signs — See a Doctor

  • Non-healing ulcer lasting more than 2 weeks
  • White patch (leukoplakia) or red patch (erythroplakia) inside mouth
  • Difficulty swallowing or persistent pain
  • Unexplained neck swelling or lumps
  • Loosening of teeth without dental cause
  • Numbness or bleeding in the mouth
⚠️

Risk Factors — Nepal / India Context

  • Tobacco chewing — gutkha, khaini, paan (highest risk)
  • Smoking — bidi carries higher risk than cigarette
  • Alcohol — synergistic effect with tobacco (multiplies risk)
  • Poor oral hygiene and sharp/broken teeth
  • HPV infection (selected cases)
  • Malnutrition and low immunity

Prognosis — Survival Rates

⬆ Early Stage (I–II)
Overall Survival 70–90%
Disease-Free 65–85%
⬇ Advanced Stage (III–IV)
Overall Survival 30–60%
Disease-Free 25–50%
✅

Early diagnosis dramatically improves survival. Stage I treated early can achieve cure rates above 85%. Don't wait.

Treatment Pathway

1
Biopsy
Tissue confirmation of cancer type and grade
2
Imaging & Staging
CT / MRI / PET scan for accurate clinical staging
3
Surgery
Wide local excision with safe margins
4
Neck Dissection
Removal of regional lymph nodes when indicated
5
Reconstruction
Restoring form, speech, and swallowing function
6
Radiotherapy / Chemotherapy
Adjuvant therapy when pathology indicates need
