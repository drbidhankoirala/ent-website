Oral Cancer Management

Oral cancer is not just a disease—it affects speech, swallowing, appearance, and dignity.

Early Diagnosis

  • Non-healing ulcer > 2 weeks
  • White/red patch (leukoplakia/erythroplakia)
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Neck swelling

Risk Factors (Indian/Nepali Context)

  • Tobacco chewing (gutkha, khaini, paan)
  • Smoking (bidi > cigarette risk)
  • Alcohol (synergistic with tobacco)
  • Poor oral hygiene
  • Sharp tooth / chronic trauma
  • HPV infection (selected cases)
  • Malnutrition

Final Staging (How It Is Done)

  • T – Tumor size & depth of invasion
  • N – Lymph node involvement
  • M – Distant metastasis

Final stage is determined AFTER surgery using histopathology (pTNM).

Prognosis (Survival)

Early Stage (I–II):

  • Overall Survival (OS): 70–90%
  • Disease-Free Survival (DFS): 65–85%

Advanced Stage (III–IV):

  • Overall Survival (OS): 30–60%
  • Disease-Free Survival (DFS): 25–50%

Early diagnosis dramatically improves survival.

Treatment

  • Biopsy
  • Surgery
  • Neck Dissection
  • Reconstruction
  • Radiotherapy / Chemotherapy

Why Consult a Head & Neck Oncosurgeon?

  • Accurate staging and decision making
  • Complete tumor removal with safe margins
  • Expert neck dissection
  • Advanced reconstruction (speech & swallowing preservation)
  • Multidisciplinary cancer care

Improper initial treatment can worsen survival and increase recurrence risk.
